## Known OpenAI Brand / Trademark Rules (inferred from public knowledge, 2026-04-29)

### 1. Name / Logo Prohibition Without Permission
OpenAI's trademarks (OpenAI, ChatGPT, Codex, GPT, DALL-E, Sora, and their
logo) may not be used in connection with third-party products or services
without written permission. This includes use in product names, marketing
materials, or listings that imply endorsement, affiliation, or sponsorship.

### 2. No "Official" or "Powered By" Without Permission
Third-party products may NOT use phrases such as "Official OpenAI partner",
"Powered by ChatGPT", "ChatGPT-powered", "Codex-enabled", or any similar
formulation without an explicit written partner-program agreement.

### 3. Correct Product-Name Capitalisation
- "OpenAI" (capital O, capital A, no space — one word stylisation)
- "ChatGPT" (capital C, capital G, capital P, T)
- "Codex" (capital C only)
- "GPT" (all caps)
- "DALL-E" (capital D, capital E, hyphen)
- "Sora" (capital S)

### 4. No Modification of Wordmarks
OpenAI's logos and wordmarks may not be altered, combined with other marks,
or used in ways that could cause brand confusion or dilution.

### 5. Attribution Requirements
If OpenAI grants a specific permission to use its marks, the attribution
requirements in the written agreement must be followed exactly.

### 6. OpenAI Partner Program
OpenAI has a "Build with OpenAI" developer program. Membership does NOT
automatically grant trademark use rights. Specific brand permissions
must be separately negotiated. As of 2026, no confirmed public brand
guidelines page exists for third-party tool builders.
