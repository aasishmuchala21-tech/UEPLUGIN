## Known Epic Games / Unreal Engine Brand Rules (inferred from public knowledge)

### 1. No "Official" or "Partner" Without Formal Agreement

Third-party developers may NOT call their UE plugins "Official Unreal
Engine plugins" or "Epic-approved" without a formal Epic Games partner
agreement. The Fab marketplace has a "Verified" or "Epic Staff Pick"
badge system that is grantable by Epic — use of any Epic badge in listing
graphics requires meeting Epic's quality bar and receiving explicit
approval. NYRA does not have this badge in v1.

### 2. Correct Product-Name Capitalisation

- "Epic Games" (two words, capital E, capital G)
- "Unreal Engine" (two words, capital U, capital E)
- "UE" (abbreviation, all caps when used as a standalone mark; no
  requirement to use ® in listing copy)
- "Unreal" (capital U)
- "Fab" (capital F — Fab is Epic's trademark)

### 3. No Modification of Epic / Unreal Engine Logos or Wordmarks

The Epic Games logo and Unreal Engine logo may not be altered, recoloured,
or combined with other marks in marketing materials. NYRA listing
graphics must not show the Unreal Engine logo or Epic Games logo without
written permission.

### 4. "Built with Unreal Engine" vs "Made in Unreal"

Epic's historically published guidelines permitted developers to say
"built with Unreal Engine" or "made with Unreal Engine" with proper
attribution, but required a formal co-marketing agreement for use of
Epic's logos. In Fab listing copy, NYRA should say "for Unreal Engine 5"
rather than using the UE logo or claiming "built with Unreal Engine"
as a branding element.

### 5. Naming Restrictions for UE Plugins

- NYRA must not call itself "Unreal Engine NYRA" or imply it is an Epic-
  owned or Epic-endorsed product.
- Plugin names on Fab must not use "UE", "Unreal", or "Epic" as a prefix
  in a way that implies official status.
- "Unreal Engine plugin" as a descriptor (noun phrase) is acceptable
  when describing what the product IS, not claiming official origin.

### 6. Fab Marketplace Naming Conventions

Fab's seller policies govern how sellers name their listings on the
marketplace. This is distinct from Epic's broader brand guidelines.
See fab-seller-branding-policy-SNAPSHOT.md for the Fab-specific rules.
The two policy surfaces interact: Epic owns Fab, so Fab's naming rules
are a subset of Epic's overall brand policy for the marketplace.

### 7. No Endorsement / Affiliation Implication

Epic Games does not endorse third-party plugins unless a formal developer
relation or co-marketing agreement is in place. NYRA's Fab listing copy
must not imply Epic's endorsement or affiliation.
